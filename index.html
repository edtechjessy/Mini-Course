Welcome to Spelling with Grace for Parents

This short, free mini-course helps parents teach spelling at home with patience and creativity. The course builds from biblical foundations, encouraging parents to affirm their child’s worth, show grace in mistakes, and design engaging materials tailored to their child’s passions. Parents will learn strategies for teaching prefixes, suffixes, high-frequency words, and tricky spelling patterns while creating fun, interest-based activities..

Who Should Take This Course?

Open to all parents and caregivers interested in supporting their child’s spelling development at home.

What Should I Expect?

Parents can expect to view short instructional videos, review printable materials, and participate in interactive activities alongside their child. Each lesson includes time for reflection, guided practice, and confidence building. To complete the course, learners need to view all lesson materials, actively engage in reflection, and finish short self-checks for each of the four lessons.

Lesson 1 — Why Children Struggle with Spelling

Welcome

Welcome to Lesson 1! In this lesson, we explore why children sometimes struggle with spelling and how you, as a parent, can respond in ways that build confidence, growth, and joy in learning.

You’ll learn about common challenges children face when learning to spell, including cognitive, linguistic, and emotional factors. Most importantly, you’ll see how biblical principles—patience, encouragement, and affirmation—can guide your interactions and make learning a positive experience.

Remember: your child is wonderfully made (Psalm 139:14). Challenges in learning do not define them—they are opportunities for you to model love, patience, and perseverance.

Common Reasons Children Struggle with Spelling

1. Phonological & Phonemic Awareness Gaps

Some children have difficulty connecting sounds to letters, which can make it challenging to spell words accurately. Activities that emphasize listening to sounds, breaking words into parts, and practicing letter-sound relationships can help.

Learn more about phonemic awareness

2. Irregular English Spelling

English has many irregular spellings. For example, “through” and “though” follow different rules for the same sounds. These inconsistencies can confuse children and make spelling slow and frustrating.

Overview of English spelling irregularities

3. Learning Differences (e.g., Dyslexia)

Some children process written language differently. Dyslexia or other learning differences can make it harder to remember spellings or apply rules consistently. Understanding your child’s unique learning profile helps you adapt your support effectively.

Dyslexia guidance from the International Dyslexia Association

Why do children sometimes struggle with spelling?

Emotional Factors

Children’s confidence and emotional state can greatly affect spelling performance. Frustration or repeated mistakes may lower self-confidence. Positive reinforcement and calm guidance help children feel safe to try and learn.

Tip: A small word of affirmation can transform a challenging moment into a confidence-building opportunity.

Simplifying Spelling Rules

Prefixes and Suffixes

Prefixes and suffixes are word parts that can help children decode and spell new words. For instance:

  • re- means “again” (e.g., redo, replay)
  • -tion often turns a verb into a noun (e.g., celebration, motion)

Lesson 4: Modeling Patience, Service, and Perseverance at Home

Welcome

Welcome to Lesson 4! In this lesson, you will explore how modeling patience, service, and perseverance supports your child’s spelling development and strengthens their character. As a parent, your actions serve as a living example, showing your child how to face challenges with calm and determination.

Reading: Understanding the Impact of Your Example

Modeling patience, service, and perseverance at home is one of the most powerful ways parents can shape their child’s learning and character. When you approach spelling practice with calm and encouragement, your child sees firsthand how to respond to challenges without frustration or fear. Scripture reminds us that our actions influence others: Philippians 1:6 assures us that God, who began a good work in our children, will continue it. By demonstrating patience, you create a safe space for your child to make mistakes and learn from them, reinforcing their confidence and perseverance in the learning process.

Service and encouragement go hand-in-hand with patience. By putting your child’s needs first during learning moments, you exemplify Christlike behavior and nurture a love for growth. Small habits, like celebrating small wins or offering gentle guidance when a child struggles with a tricky word, model perseverance and dedication. Even when you feel frustrated, taking a moment to reflect, pray, or use a prepared script shows your child the importance of self-control and consistency. Over time, these examples help your child internalize positive learning habits and understand that success comes through effort, persistence, and a supportive environment.
